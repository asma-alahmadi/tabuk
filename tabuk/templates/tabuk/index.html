About Tabuk

The Tabuk region combines miscellaneous geographical and topographical elements of seas, plains, mountains and ancient antiquities, as well as wonderful fair weather all year long, which made a tourist destination that attracts the residents and guests of the region during the summer, in addition to annual festivals that are under the supervision of the branch of the Saudi Commission for Tourism and National Heritage in the region. Tabuk is located between An Nafud Desert from the east and the Red Sea from the west, while five of its governorates are located on the Red Sea coast, which are the governorates of Haqil, Diba’, Al Wajh, Amlaj and Al Bad’; An Nafud dunes are considered the most distinguished feature of the Taima’ Governorate.

Tabuk... A Destination for Seekers of History and Sea

Tabuk City is known for its being one of the most important and biggest cities in the northern side of the Kingdom, and is characterized by authenticity and elegance. The establishment of the city dates back to 500 BC, and it has had many names over the years, and it has been known by its current name since the early years of Islam until now; among its most famous titles are the Tabuk of roses and the gate of the north.

The Tabuk Region is famous for being a major agricultural area in the Kingdom and agriculture is mostly centred in the Tabuk City in particular, where some 70% of the cultivated areas in the region can be found in Tabuk City and its suburbs, which can be attributed to the abundance of water in Tabuk, where the city is main source for water in the entire region due to its geographical nature that is known for its ability to store water. Among the most important agricultural produce in Tabuk are wheat, fruits and vegetables, as well as roses that are marketed locally and internationally, where roses are exported to some countries of the world. The region is also home to a big number of archaeological sites, mainly in the Tabuk City, including the Tawbah Mosque.
